STORIES BEHIND THE GRAFFITI

BOZZG

"My art is a reflection of who I am. Street art is nothing else but urban poetry that catches someone’s eye. People may see my artwork as tagging and vadelism, but through my perspective I see nothing but creativity, passion and beauty. TAG is like my second home. A safe space for creativity."

SPRAYZ

When I was little I was so fixated about street graffiti and how it made the environment more colourful and creative. Ever since I have been in TAG it's been nothing but a great expperience to see everyone's amazing work and how someones creativity can be so unimaginable. Graffiti is a hobbie that I usually go to when I can't think straight. My artwork displays my expressions and how I feel in that moment.

GRAPHGIRL

I discovered my passion for street graffiti when I was 15 and started painting fences. Ever since TAG came along in my life I've stayed out of trouble and just worked hard on my artwork. TAG made a massive difference in my life. Found people who I can connect with. Changed my life.

About

  • What We Do

    TAG also know as Talk About your Graffiti is a closed group where artist can express and use their passion for painting, drawing etc within the group. We are located in Wellington Underground where our Art studio is. A safe space for young artist to show their raw talents. When the artist are ready to present, they go out in the environment and find a space where they feel comfortable with and starts creating.

  • Invites

    We have our team who are in charge of the whole system. As they go and discover new artist they create a username for you as you are respected into the group. Every closed group has a sign and ours is "red marks the spot" If we see that something is wrong they paint a red cross near them so we know where they are. The code is a system of warning. If there is Polices around we help as a family and cover for the artist.

Phlegm in the

Known for his intricate large-scale black and white paintings, Sheffield-based artist Phlegm began as an illustrator, producing his own handmade comics and zines. Eventually he began to reproduce the fantastical worlds of his imagination on a larger scale, transforming the walls of Sheffield’s decaying steel factories and warehouses.

  • Painter: Phlegm
